The Whale Returns: DeepSeek V4 Brings Frontier Capability on Huawei Chips

DeepSeek pushed its V4 preview live with two open-source variants: V4-Pro at 1.6T parameters with 49B activated and V4-Flash at 284B with 13B activated, both supporting one million-token context windows under the MIT license. The new Compressed Sparse Attention plus Heavily Compressed Attention design slashes inference cost to 27% of V3.2 FLOPs and 10% of KV cache at long context. The bigger story: Huawei's Ascend chips now natively support V4 inference, giving China a working domestic alternative to the Nvidia stack. V4-Pro lands at $1.74 / $3.48 per million input/output tokens, well below GPT-5.5 at $5/$30 and Opus 4.7 at $5/$25.

#3

Anthropic's Project Deal Lets Claude Agents Trade for 69 Employees

Anthropic published results from Project Deal, a one-week experiment where Claude agents handled buying and selling for 69 employees in a private Slack marketplace, completing 186 deals worth more than $4,000. Identical items fetched $3.64 more under Opus agents, with one folding bike selling for $65 via Opus but only $38 via Haiku. The kicker: Haiku users still rated their deals 4.06 / 7 for fairness, statistically tied with Opus users at 4.05, meaning users on weaker models did not notice they were being out-negotiated. Nearly half said they would pay for the service, and Anthropic warned that "policy and legal frameworks" for agent commerce do not yet exist.
