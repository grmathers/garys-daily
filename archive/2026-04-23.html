#3

Sony's Ace Robot Beats Elite Table Tennis Players on a Full-Size Court

Sony AI's Ace is the first robotic system to defeat elite human players at table tennis, using nine high-speed cameras to triangulate the ball in 3D and three gaze-control rigs to read spin mid-flight. The system reacts in 20 ms, about 10x faster than a human, feeding spin and trajectory data into a deep reinforcement learning policy trained entirely in simulation over roughly 3,000 hours of self-play. By December it was beating professionals, including an unreturnable backspin shot that stumped 1992 Olympian Kinjiro Nakamura courtside. Table tennis has long been robotics' hardest perception-motor loop, and the architecture behind Ace is exactly what higher-stakes robotic environments need.
