Google Will Sell Its TPU Chips to Outside Customers, Squaring Up Against Nvidia

Alphabet is preparing to sell its custom Tensor Processing Units to a select set of customers who would install them in their own data centers, a sharp escalation of Google's chip ambitions. Zuckerberg's Biohub Pours $500M Into AI for Biology

The Chan Zuckerberg-backed Biohub announced a $500M, five-year Virtual Biology Initiative to build the open datasets and models needed to predict how human cells behave. $400M will fund data generation and imaging tech, with $100M flowing to outside research labs. #3

Mayo Clinic's REDMOD Spots Pancreatic Cancer Up to Three Years Early

Mayo Clinic published landmark validation data on REDMOD, an AI that reads invisible texture and structure patterns on standard CT scans and catches pancreatic cancer up to three years before doctors typically find it. Reviewing nearly 2,000 routine CT scans that specialists had originally read as normal, REDMOD picked up 73% of cases early and spotted roughly 3x as many cancers at the two-year mark as experienced radiologists. Pancreatic cancer's five-year survival rate sits below 15%, so an AI that runs on scans patients already get could fold early screening into routine care without adding a single new appointment.

Apple Research Unveils LaDiR for Latent-Diffusion Reasoning

Apple's machine learning team published LaDiR (Latent Diffusion Reasoner), a framework that pairs continuous latent representations with the iterative refinement of latent diffusion to upgrade an existing LLM. LaDiR consistently improves accuracy, diversity, and interpretability over autoregressive and prior latent reasoning baselines.
