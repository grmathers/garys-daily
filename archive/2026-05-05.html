Peter Thiel Backs $140M Round to Float AI Data Centers in the Pacific

Oregon-based Panthalassa just raised a $140M Series B led by Peter Thiel at a near-$1B valuation, building autonomous 85-meter steel compute "nodes" that bob in open ocean, convert wave motion into electricity for onboard AI chips, cool naturally with seawater, and beam results back via Starlink. The funding finishes a pilot factory near Portland and deploys the first wave-powered nodes in the Pacific, with commercial rollout targeted for 2027. AI data center construction has become one of the most contentious public flashpoints of 2026, and with space-based options still years away, the ocean is suddenly the most credible place to put new AI compute that nobody local has to live next to.

Lenny: Why SaaS Freemium Playbooks Don't Work in AI, and What Actually Does

Vikas Kansal of Google AI lays out the three pillars for building a sustainable AI paywall: gate usage intensity (Plus/Pro/Ultra tiers tied to compute volume), gate outcomes ("sell hours, not answers" — Intercom's Fin AI charges $0.99 per resolution), and gate the heaviest compute modalities (cinematic video and real-time world models go in the top tier). Then bundle with sticky utilities like cloud storage, route prompts intelligently to cheap models when possible, and avoid peak-agnostic flat pricing.

What 628 AI-Generated Email Summaries Taught Us About PR Pitches

After analyzing 628 AI-generated email summaries, BuzzStream found that 82-87% of summary content comes from the first half of the message, so lead with your key points. Tight copy and bullet points get pulled in more reliably. Subject-line keywords like "new," "data," and expert titles end up shaping up to 60% of summaries. Critical info should live in the body, not in tables or attachments. Expect AI to misrepresent content roughly 33% of the time and double-check coverage manually.
